# Customer Journey Map for Silicon-Infer

## Phase 1: Aware
- **Trigger Event**: Developers hear about the limitations of existing coding models on Apple Silicon hardware through forums, social media, or tech blogs.
- **Friction Points**: Lack of awareness about local coding models optimized for Apple Silicon; misinformation about performance capabilities.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration due to slow inference times and dependency on cloud solutions; curiosity about alternatives.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Create engaging content (blogs, videos) showcasing the performance of Silicon-Infer; leverage testimonials from early users.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits and engagement metrics on awareness content (e.g., video views, blog shares).

## Phase 2: Consider
- **Trigger Event**: Developers actively research local coding models and compare options.
- **Friction Points**: Overwhelming amount of information; difficulty in finding reliable comparisons; skepticism about performance claims.
- **User Emotions**: Confusion and cautious optimism; desire for validation from peers.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide clear comparison charts, case studies, and performance benchmarks; offer a community forum for discussions.
- **Metric**: Number of downloads of comparison materials and engagement in community forums.

## Phase 3: Try
- **Trigger Event**: Developers decide to test Silicon-Infer after hearing positive feedback from peers or seeing compelling performance data.
- **Friction Points**: Installation challenges; initial setup complexity; lack of clear documentation.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with anxiety about the setup process; hope for a seamless experience.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer a guided installation process, interactive tutorials, and responsive support during the trial phase.
- **Metric**: Trial downloads and user feedback scores on the installation experience.

## Phase 4: Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: Developers successfully integrate Silicon-Infer into their workflow and experience improved performance.
- **Friction Points**: Potential bugs or performance issues; integration with existing tools may not be seamless.
- **User Emotions**: Satisfaction from improved performance; relief from previous frustrations; pride in using cutting-edge technology.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Regular updates and quick fixes; proactive communication about new features and enhancements; personalized onboarding sessions.
- **Metric**: User retention rate and frequency of use metrics (e.g., daily active users).

## Phase 5: Expand
- **Trigger Event**: Developers recognize the value of Silicon-Infer and consider using it for additional projects or recommending it to peers.
- **Friction Points**: Limited awareness of advanced features; potential hesitance to invest in additional licenses or features.
- **User Emotions**: Enthusiasm for the product’s capabilities; desire to share success stories; cautious about additional costs.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer loyalty discounts for referrals or additional licenses; create a user community to share best practices and success stories.
- **Metric**: Number of referrals and upsell conversions to premium features or licenses.
